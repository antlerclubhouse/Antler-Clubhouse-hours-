Schedule C vs. Schedule E: If your average guest stay is 7 days or less (typical for STRs), the IRS usually treats this as a business reported on Schedule C, not rental income on Schedule E. The expense categories below work for either — your CPA picks the form. Depreciation is intentionally left for your CPA to calculate.
Import a statement or order report

Download a CSV from your Fifth Third checking or credit card (Account → Statements/Activity → Export/Download as CSV), then load it here. You'll map the columns and assign categories before anything is saved. Nothing leaves your browser.

In Amazon: Account → Order History Reports (or "Download order reports"), choose Items or Retail order history, pick your date range, and download the CSV. Load it here — each line item comes in as its own transaction with the product name, so you categorize what's a supply vs. a repair vs. a depreciable asset. The columns auto-map; just confirm.

Why this beats screenshots: the order report itemizes purchases that hit your card as one lump charge, with real product names as text. Heads up — Amazon items still land in Needs review on purpose, because only you can tell a $14 pack of coffee (Supplies) from a $420 mattress (a depreciable asset, not an expense). The product name in each row helps you decide fast.

Material participation — supporting evidence

Transactions you've tagged to an owner activity. This is a documentation aid: each row is a dated record that an activity happened, to back up the hours you log elsewhere. It does not count or calculate hours.

Read this: This tab does not log or total hours, and bookkeeping time is generally not material participation. Keep your actual hours in your separate tracker. Whether an activity qualifies, and which participation test applies, is a determination for your CPA — these tagged transactions are just evidence to support it.
Auto-categorization rules

When you import a statement, each rule looks at the raw description (and optionally an exact amount) and sets the category & note automatically. The raw description is never changed. Anything no rule matches lands in Needs review.

Why amount matters for Venmo: Fifth Third's Venmo lines don't include the payee name (just "VENMO PAYMENT" + a per-transaction reference number that changes every time). So matching "VENMO PAYMENT" alone would catch every Venmo payment. Adding the exact amount ($225 for Kellie's standard clean) makes it specific. Non-$225 Venmo payments stay in review.
